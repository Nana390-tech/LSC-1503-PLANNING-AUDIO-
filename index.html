🎙️ Audio Presentation Plan

Prepare a short audio overview of your TED-style career presentation before your final exam

⏱️ 3–5 minutes
🎤 Teams Assignment
⭐ 5% of Grade
📅 Plan Stage
🎙️

What is this activity?

Before your final presentation, you will record a short audio overview of your plan on Microsoft Teams. This is NOT your full presentation — it is a planning summary that shows your teacher you are prepared.

💡

This is a PLAN, not a performance

You do not need to present your whole topic. Just explain your ideas — what you plan to say, how your slides will look, and why you made your choices. Think of it like telling a friend what your presentation is about.

📋

What to cover in your audio

Your 3–5 minute recording should briefly touch on all five parts below.

1

🪝 Your Hook

Tell your teacher how you plan to open your presentation. What will you say or do in the first 30 seconds to grab attention?

2

📖 Your Story

What personal story connects you to this career topic? Why does it matter to you?

3

📌 Your Main Points

Briefly mention your 2–3 key points. What information will you share about your chosen career?

4

🎨 Your PowerPoint Design

Explain your slide design choices — the colours, layout, font, and images — and where you found your visuals.

5

🗣️ Your Preparation

How are you preparing to deliver this? Practice methods, what you still need to work on, or how you are managing nerves.

🌟

Why does this matter?

This recording counts as 5% of your overall grade. More importantly, it helps you organise your thoughts before the final exam, and your teacher can give you feedback to help you improve before presentation day.

Your Audio Script Guide

Use these example phrases and sentence starters to help you record naturally. You do NOT need to use these exact words — they are just a guide to help you get started.

⏱️

Target time: 3–5 minutes total

Spend roughly 30–60 seconds on each of the five sections. You do not need to memorise a script — speaking naturally from notes is perfectly fine and actually sounds better!

1
Opening — Introduce yourself & your topic
Start by greeting your teacher and telling them your presentation topic.
"Hi, my name is [name], and I am a student in LSC 1503. For my final presentation, I have chosen to talk about a career in [topic]."
💬 Keep this simple — just your name and your topic.
"In this audio recording, I will give you an overview of my presentation plan — my hook, my story, my main points, and my PowerPoint design."
💬 This tells the listener what to expect — like a mini roadmap.
My topic is…
I decided to talk about…
My presentation is about…
2
Part 1 — My Hook 🪝
Explain how you plan to open your presentation to grab your audience's attention in the first 30 seconds.
"To start my presentation, I am planning to open with a surprising statistic. I will say something like: 'Did you know that [career] is expected to grow by 25% in the next 10 years?' I chose this hook because I think it will immediately make the audience wonder why this career is growing so fast."
💬 Explain your hook TYPE and WHY you chose it. Hook types include: a question, a statistic, a short story, a bold statement, or a quote.
"I am going to start with a personal question to the audience: 'Have you ever wondered what it feels like to [do this job every day]?' I hope this makes them think about the topic right away."
I plan to open with…
My hook will be…
I chose this hook because…
I think this will grab attention because…
3
Part 2 — My Personal Story 📖
Briefly describe the personal story or connection that links you to this career topic.
"After my hook, I will share a short personal story about why I chose this topic. When I was in school, I had an experience that made me interested in [career field]… I will not tell the whole story right now, but I will connect it to my main message: [one sentence summary of your message]."
💬 You don't need to tell the full story here — just describe it briefly and explain how it connects to your career topic.
"I chose this topic because someone in my family works in [this field], and I have always been curious about what it really involves. I will use my personal connection to make the presentation feel more real to the audience."
My personal connection is…
I will share a story about…
This topic matters to me because…
My story is about…
4
Part 3 — My Main Points 📌
Mention your 2–3 key points about the career. Keep this brief — just one sentence per point.
"My presentation will have three main points. First, I will explain what a [job title] actually does every day. Second, I will talk about the skills and qualifications needed. Third, I will share information about the job market — how many opportunities there are in the UAE and what the salary range looks like."
💬 Use "First… Second… Third…" to organise your points clearly.
"My three key points are: the responsibilities of this career, the required education and training, and the future growth of this field. I chose these because I think they are the most important things my audience needs to understand about this job."
My first point is…
I will cover three topics…
My main points are…
I chose these points because…
5
Part 4 — My PowerPoint Design 🎨
This is important for your oral defence! Explain your slide design choices and where you got your images.
"For my PowerPoint design, I chose a clean, professional theme with a dark blue and white colour scheme. I chose these colours because they look modern and professional, which matches the serious tone of my topic. I used simple fonts so the text is easy to read, and I kept each slide uncluttered with only 3–4 bullet points."
"For my images, I sourced them from Unsplash and Pexels — both are free, copyright-free websites. I made sure all images were relevant to each slide and high quality. I avoided using Google Images directly because the copyright is not always clear."
💬 Mention WHERE you got your images! Good sources: Unsplash, Pexels, Canva, or photos you took yourself.
I chose this design because…
My colour scheme is… because…
I got my images from…
I kept my slides simple because…
6
Part 5 — My Preparation & Closing 🗣️
End your recording by briefly explaining how you are preparing and what you still plan to work on.
"To prepare for my presentation, I am planning to practise in front of a mirror at home and record myself once to check my timing and pronunciation. I also wrote notes on small cards so I can remember the key points without reading from the slides."
"One thing I still need to work on is making eye contact and speaking more slowly when I am nervous. But overall, I feel quite prepared and I am looking forward to presenting."
"That is the end of my audio plan. Thank you for listening, and I look forward to your feedback."
💬 Always close politely — this is professional communication!
To prepare, I am…
I plan to practise by…
One challenge I have is…
Thank you for listening.

Recording Tips

Find a quiet room. Speak at a normal pace — not too fast. It's okay to pause and think. You can record in one take or record sections separately. Do NOT read word-for-word — speak naturally from notes.

How to Submit on Microsoft Teams

Your teacher has created an Assignment for you on Teams. Follow these steps to record and submit your audio.

Step-by-Step Guide
1

Open Microsoft Teams

Log in with your HCT student account. Go to your LSC 1503 class channel.

2

Find the Assignment

Click on Assignments in the top menu. Look for the assignment titled "Audio Presentation Plan" (or as named by your teacher).

3

Record Your Audio

Inside the assignment, click Add work → Video / Audio recording. Teams will open a recording tool directly in the app. Allow microphone access when prompted.

4

Make Your Recording

Speak clearly and cover your five sections: Hook → Story → Main Points → Design → Preparation. Aim for 3–5 minutes. You can re-record if needed.

5

Review & Attach

Play back your recording once to check the audio quality is clear. If you are happy, attach it to the assignment. You can also attach any notes you used.

6

Submit ✅

Click Turn in. You will see a confirmation screen. Do this before the deadline your teacher has set.

🎧

Audio quality tips

Use earphones with a microphone for best quality. Record in a quiet room — a bedroom or study area works well. Avoid recording near a fan or open window. Speak close to the microphone but not directly into it.

Alternative: Record Outside Teams First

If you prefer to record using your phone's voice memo app first, that is also fine.

A

Record on your phone

Use your phone's Voice Memos app (iPhone) or Sound Recorder (Android).

B

Save the file

Export as .m4a or .mp3 — both are accepted.

C

Upload to Teams Assignment

In the Assignment, click Add work → Upload from device → select your audio file → Turn in.

⚠️

Important reminders

Submit before the deadline. Check that your name can be heard clearly at the start of your recording. Late submissions may affect your grade — contact your teacher early if you have a problem.
